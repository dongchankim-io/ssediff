## Notes for the Reviewer
- `Close()` does **not** emit ORPHANs for items still buffered at shutdown — those are intentionally discarded because the operator is shutting down anyway and a flood of orphans on the way down is operator noise, not signal. If you disagree, that's a defensible different choice and I'll revise.
- The non-blocking send inside `tryPublishLocked` is the back-pressure release valve. Drops are counted (`droppedCount`) and exposed via `Stats()`; spec §3.6 wires this into `/api/stats` in Slice 004.
- `gjson` extraction happens **inside** the matcher's lock for design simplicity. If profiling later shows extraction is the bottleneck, moving it outside the lock is mechanical (it's a read-only op on a byte slice).
